Local Tech Startup Revolutionizes Healthcare Access

ADELAIDE, March 10, 2025

A new healthcare platform developed by Adelaide-based startup HealthBridgei is transforming how regional communities access specialized medical care.

The platform, which launched last month after securing $3.2 million in funding from Southbank Ventures, connects patients in remote areas with specialists through secure video consultations and AI-powered preliminary assessments.

"We're solving a critical problem that affects millions of Australians living outside major cities," said Sarah Chen, CEO of HealthBridge. "When the nearest specialist might be 300 kilometers away, many health conditions go untreated or are diagnosed too late."

The company's platform integrates with existing electronic health record systems provided by MediSoft Solutions and has already been adopted by Regional Health Networki, which operates 12 hospitals across South Australia and the Northern Territory.

Dr. James Morton, Chief Medical Officer at Regional Health Network, highlighted the platform's impact: "In just the first month, we've facilitated over 500 specialist consultations that would have otherwise required patients to travel to Adelaide or Darwin. The cost savings and improved patient outcomes are significant."

HealthBridge developed the technology using secure infrastructure from Australian data services provider SecureCloud, ensuring all patient information remains protected and compliant with Australian healthcare data regulations.

The South Australian government has expressed interest in expanding the program statewide through its Digital Health Initiative, potentially bringing specialized healthcare access to over 400,000 residents in remote communities.

"This is exactly the kind of innovation we need to see in Australian healthcare," said Michael Thompson, health policy analyst at Australian Healthcare Review. "It combines technical innovation with practical solutions to real problems facing our healthcare system."

HealthBridge plans to expand to other states by the end of 2025 and is currently developing additional features including integration with wearable health monitoring devices and automated prescription services.
